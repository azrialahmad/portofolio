# CLAUDE.md — Azrial's Portfolio

## Design Context

### Users
Primarily recruiters and fellow developers visiting to evaluate Azrial as a candidate for data engineering, ML engineering, QA, or full-stack roles. Secondary: creative peers from photography/design communities. They need to quickly understand who Azrial is, what he can build, and what kind of person he is.

### Brand Personality
**Curious. Minimalist. Expressive.**
- Voice: Direct, considered, not trying too hard
- Tone: Authentic — a fresh grad with genuine breadth (not a generalist in a bad way)
- Emotional goal: Leave visitors thinking "this person is thoughtful and multi-dimensional"

### Aesthetic Direction
Inspired by editorial magazine design — specifically the **blue-on-cream** palette with elegant serif/cursive display headlines mixed with clean body type. Think independent arts publication meets technical portfolio.
- **Primary palette**: Rich blue (oklch-based, deep and saturated, not electric/electric cyan) on warm off-white/cream
- **Dark mode**: Deep navy/slate with cream text — same editorial feel, not neon-on-black
- **Images**: Blue duotone treatment for consistency across photography sections
- **Typography**: Elegant serif/italic display face for big moments + neutral geometric sans for body
- **Anti-references**: No purple-to-blue gradients, no glassmorphism, no neon glow effects, no "generic AI dark portfolio" look

### Design Principles
1. **Editorial restraint** — white space is not empty, it's intentional. Less is more, every element earns its place.
2. **Blue as a character** — blue is not just an accent, it IS the brand. It duotones photos, it defines type weight, it draws the eye.
3. **Human in the technical** — the site should express that Azrial is curious and creative, not just a list of skills
4. **Asymmetric rhythm** — avoid center-everything layouts; use left-aligned, offset compositions that feel designed
5. **Photography as identity** — integrate street/candid and cosplay portrait work as a natural expression of the same eye that writes good code
